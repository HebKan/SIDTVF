# Employee Monitoring Notice Template

> **Instructions:** This template provides a starting point for an employee monitoring notice. It must be reviewed and customized by your Legal counsel before use. Requirements vary significantly by jurisdiction — particularly in EU member states, California, New York, and Illinois. This template is not legal advice.
>
> **Delivery:** This notice should be delivered at onboarding (before first system access), when material changes are made to monitoring scope, and as a periodic acknowledgment (at least annually). Keep a record of employee acknowledgments.

---

# Notice of Electronic Monitoring and Acceptable Use

**[Organization Name]**
Effective Date: [Date] | Version: [1.0]

---

## 1. Purpose of This Notice

[Organization Name] ("the Company") monitors the use of its information systems, networks, and devices to:

- Protect the security of the Company's information, systems, and operations
- Detect unauthorized access, data loss, fraud, and other security incidents
- Comply with applicable laws and regulatory requirements
- Enforce compliance with Company policies

This notice explains what the Company monitors, why it monitors, and your rights in connection with that monitoring.

---

## 2. What Is Monitored

The following activities on Company-owned or Company-managed systems are subject to monitoring:

| System / Activity | What Is Monitored |
|------------------|------------------|
| **Corporate email** ([domain]) | Metadata (sender, recipient, subject, timestamps), attachments, message content when required by investigation |
| **Corporate network and internet access** | Websites visited, data volumes, connection times, destination addresses |
| **Endpoint devices** (laptops, workstations) | Application usage, file access and transfer activity, device configuration changes |
| **Cloud services** ([list corporate SaaS tools]) | Login events, file access, sharing, downloads, configuration changes |
| **Database and application systems** | Login events, queries executed, data accessed or exported |
| **Remote access (VPN)** | Connection times, source IP addresses, systems accessed |
| **Physical access systems** | Badge swipe records for building and secure area access |

**This monitoring applies to activities on Company systems regardless of whether those activities occur during business hours or outside business hours, and regardless of whether those activities occur on Company premises or remotely.**

---

## 3. What Is Not Monitored

The Company does not monitor:
- Personal email accounts (Gmail, Yahoo, etc.) accessed on personal devices
- Personal cloud storage accessed on personal devices not enrolled in Company MDM
- Personal social media accounts
- Communications protected by attorney-client privilege, subject to applicable law
- [Any other carve-outs your Legal team specifies]

**Note for BYOD users:** If your personal device is enrolled in the Company's Mobile Device Management (MDM) system, the Company may monitor work-related applications and data on that device. Personal applications and data on a BYOD device are not monitored. Consult the BYOD Policy for details.

---

## 4. No Expectation of Privacy

You should have **no expectation of privacy** when using Company systems, networks, or devices for any purpose. This applies to communications and activities you may consider personal, such as personal emails sent using your corporate email account or personal browsing conducted using the Company network or devices.

The absence of monitoring on any given occasion does not create an expectation of privacy.

---

## 5. Purpose Limitation

Data collected through monitoring is used for the purposes described in Section 1. It is not used for:
- General performance evaluation unrelated to policy compliance
- Marketing or commercial purposes
- Disclosure to third parties except as required by law, legal process, or the Company's legal obligations

---

## 6. Legal Basis for Monitoring

**United States:** Monitoring is conducted pursuant to the Electronic Communications Privacy Act (ECPA), 18 U.S.C. § 2511(2)(d), which permits monitoring by system operators and where employees have consented. Your acknowledgment of this notice constitutes consent to the described monitoring activities.
